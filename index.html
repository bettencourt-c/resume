Laboratory Professional Experience

Takeda Pharmaceuticals: Research Associate, Protein Biophysical Analytics Co-op

January 2024 - June 2024

The core focus of my role within the Global Biologics team was in vitro high-throughput plate-based flow cytometry assay screens for the selection and optimization of drug candidates. I also developed an immunogenicity risk assessment assay as well as an FcRn binding assay. I eventually combined separate assay tools to create a single, more robust assay and consequently used my data to build a use-case for proceeding forward with commercial assay development.

CAMP4 Therapeutics: Research Associate, Discovery II Co-op, Preclinical

January 2023 - August 2023

I aided in assay development for the quantification of antisense oligonucleotides in various biological matrices. Eventually, I transitioned to high throughput screening to develop pharmacokinetic profiles that were used for Investigational New Drug applications. As the company prepared for their first regulatory filing, I began doing quality control for regulatory documents due to my attention to detail and ability to work under tight deadlines. I concluded my co-op by presenting the newly validated methods and resulting pharmacokinetic profiles completed by our group to the R&D team.

American College of Thessaloniki: Undergraduate Research Assistant

March 2022 - May 2022

My group analyzed the antibacterial activity of a propolis extract, a compound commonly used in the local region as an antibacterial astringent, against bacteria commonly found on human skin. We worked collaboratively through key aspects of microbiology and aseptic techniques, such as Kirby-Bauer assays. Our research found preliminary evidence that propolis inhibited bacterial growth, and may work synergistically with antibiotics.
